WHO IMCI BASIC TRIAGE NOTES

General danger signs in children:
- Not able to drink or breastfeed
- Vomits everything
- Convulsions
- Lethargic or unconscious
- Severe respiratory distress

Fever assessment:
- Check duration of fever
- Check for rash
- Check for danger signs
- If fever with danger signs, refer urgently
- If fever without danger signs, assess for localizing signs and hydration

Diarrhoea assessment:
- Look for dehydration
- General condition: lethargic, restless, irritable
- Sunken eyes
- Ability to drink
- Skin pinch goes back very slowly
- Severe dehydration requires urgent referral

Cough or difficult breathing:
- Count respiratory rate
- Look for chest indrawing
- Look for stridor in calm child
- Danger signs or severe breathing difficulty require urgent referral

Malnutrition and anaemia:
- Visible severe wasting
- Oedema of both feet
- Palmar pallor
- Severe acute malnutrition needs urgent attention

Ear problems:
- Ear pain
- Ear discharge
- Tender swelling behind ear

Basic triage principles:
- Red zone: urgent danger signs, immediate referral
- Yellow zone: moderate risk, needs treatment and close follow-up
- Green zone: mild symptoms, home care with safety advice

Always provide safety advice:
- Return immediately if child becomes unable to drink, develops convulsions, becomes unusually sleepy, or breathing worsens.
